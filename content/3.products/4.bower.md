## Challenges transforming large, heteroegenous data estates into insights

Organisations accumulate vast collections of documents, datasets, spatial files and structured records across decades — typically spread across disconnected storage systems, in mixed formats, with inconsistent metadata and no unified way to search or govern them. As AI tools mature, the opportunity to extract value from this data is significant, but most organisations lack the integration infrastructure to make that possible safely and reliably.

- **Mixed-format data at scale** — thousands of documents, spreadsheets, spatial files and images with no common structure or catalogue
- **No unified search** — staff can't find what they need across silos; natural-language querying across the full data estate is impossible without AI integration
- **AI without grounding produces unreliable answers** — large language models hallucinate when there is no Knowledge Graph providing factual context about your specific domain
- **Lock-in risk** — proprietary AI platforms bind you to a single model provider, preventing adoption of better tools as they emerge
- **Data sovereignty concerns** — sending sensitive organisational data to external AI services creates privacy, sovereignty and compliance risks

::KCard
Knowledge Graphs are the secret behind the most reliable search and AI products. In the generative AI era, a domain Knowledge Graph provides the factual grounding that gives you confidence in the answers you receive — connecting AI outputs directly to your own authoritative data.
#title
Knowledge Graphs as the key to trustworthy AI
::

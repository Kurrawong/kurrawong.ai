## Problem space

Organisations accumulate vast collections of documents, datasets, spatial files and structured records across decades — typically spread across disconnected storage systems, in mixed formats, with inconsistent metadata and no unified way to search or govern them. As AI tools mature, the opportunity to extract value from this data is significant, but most organisations lack the integration infrastructure to make that possible safely and reliably.

::KGrid{class="grid-cols-1 md:grid-cols-2"}


::KCard
Thousands of documents, spreadsheets, spatial files and images with no common structure or catalogue
#title
⚡ Mixed-format data at scale
::  
  
::KCard
Staff can't find what they need across silos; natural-language querying across the full data estate can be ineffective without AI integration
#title
🔍 No unified search
::  
  
::KCard
Large language model (LLM) responses relevance and accuracy varies greatly when there is no Knowledge Graph providing factual context about your specific domain
#title
🔗 AI without grounding produces unreliable answers
::  
  
::KCard
Proprietary AI platforms bind you to a single model provider, preventing adoption of better tools as they emerge
#title
🔄 Lock-in risk
::  
  
::KCard
Sending sensitive organisational data to external AI services creates privacy, sovereignty and compliance risks
#title
🔒 Data sovereignty concerns
